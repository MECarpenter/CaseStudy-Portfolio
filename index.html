Field & Feast

A digital rebrand project by Maria Carpenter

← Back to Case Studies

Overview

Field & Feast is a complete brand redesign focused on creating a cohesive and sustainable identity for a farm-to-table business. The project emphasizes organic textures, fresh imagery, and a digital experience that communicates authenticity, local sourcing, and community connection. This case study explores the design process from initial research through final implementation.

Problem

The existing Field & Feast brand lacked visual consistency and failed to represent the company’s values. Its color palette and type hierarchy didn’t align with its audience expectations. The challenge was to modernize the design while maintaining a natural, rustic tone that resonated with the brand’s sustainable mission.

Goal

Develop a digital identity that reflects a fresh, eco-friendly image while maintaining approachability and warmth. The design needed to appeal to environmentally conscious consumers while elevating the brand’s visual storytelling across web and print.

Design Process

The process began with a deep dive into competitor analysis and visual moodboards to define a modern yet organic look. I developed color swatches based on earth tones — greens, browns, and neutrals — paired with rounded, clean typography. The logo redesign emphasized simplicity and readability, while supporting graphics such as leaf patterns and rustic textures enhanced brand cohesion.

Solutions

The final design included a responsive web layout, updated color scheme, and an adaptable logo suite. Each element was tested for accessibility and legibility, ensuring visual harmony across platforms. Additional deliverables included mockups for packaging, a social media style guide, and presentation materials.

Outcome

The rebrand successfully captured the brand’s essence of sustainability and community connection. The refreshed aesthetic improved brand recognition and user engagement metrics. Feedback from peers and instructors highlighted the project’s strong color harmony, consistent tone, and accessible design choices.

Reflection

This project strengthened my understanding of brand identity systems and the importance of consistency across digital and physical materials. It also refined my workflow in Figma and Adobe Illustrator, enhancing my ability to translate client values into visual form. The biggest takeaway was learning to balance creativity with clarity — ensuring beauty never compromises usability.

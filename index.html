Vanguard

Interactive Design for Modern Storytelling

Background

The Vanguard project demonstrates the importance of hierarchy, rhythm, and movement in modern web design. The goal of this assignment was to explore how these design principles guide the user’s attention while maintaining accessibility and structure. This project helped me develop a stronger understanding of layout balance and the use of motion to enhance user experience rather than distract from it.

Problem / Objective

The main objective was to create a web page that reflects professionalism and creativity while showcasing strong design and coding skills. The challenge was to effectively use hierarchy and animation to build a cohesive and visually engaging layout that remains user-friendly and accessible.

Process & Methodology

I started by sketching and planning the layout, focusing on how each section could flow naturally for the viewer. I used semantic HTML5 and CSS Grid to structure the page, ensuring that the design was both responsive and organized. CSS transitions and hover effects were applied to add movement and depth. I tested the site for accessibility and device responsiveness throughout development to maintain consistency and usability.

Results & Outcome

The final result was a modern and interactive web page that demonstrates mastery of layout structure, motion, and design rhythm. The page achieved visual harmony while maintaining accessibility standards, resulting in a professional and engaging experience for users.

Reflection

Vanguard reinforced my appreciation for the relationship between motion, hierarchy, and accessibility in web design. It allowed me to experiment with creative elements while maintaining focus on function and usability. This project reflects my ability to integrate thoughtful design with purposeful interaction.

Field & Feast

Farm-to-Table Design and Brand Concept

Background

Field & Feast is a conceptual website project that highlights farm-to-table values and organic dining experiences. The goal of the assignment was to create a clean and welcoming layout that reflects sustainability and nature-inspired aesthetics. The design needed to communicate freshness, warmth, and authenticity through typography and color choices.

Problem / Objective

The objective was to design a user-friendly, visually appealing website that represents local farming and community connection. The challenge was to balance elegance with simplicity and ensure the interface felt approachable and authentic to the brand’s organic mission.

Process & Methodology

The design process began with color palette exploration and wireframing. I used warm earth tones and soft neutral backgrounds to evoke a natural feeling. Semantic HTML and CSS Grid were used to organize the layout clearly. Attention was placed on spacing, imagery, and typography to maintain readability and flow. Accessibility testing was done to ensure color contrast and responsive adaptability across devices.

Results & Outcome

The final layout successfully captures the brand’s wholesome identity and local charm. The cohesive use of color, typography, and imagery conveys freshness and authenticity. The design is responsive and performs smoothly on mobile and desktop screens, reflecting professional attention to detail.

Reflection

Field & Feast helped me improve my ability to design for emotional tone and brand consistency. It taught me how minimalism and color psychology can shape user perception and strengthen brand storytelling.

Wunderlust Travel

Accessible, Mobile-First Travel Website

Background

The Wunderlust project was developed to demonstrate the principles of responsive, accessible web design. The goal was to create a clean, travel-themed website that adapts to multiple devices and provides a user-friendly browsing experience for planning trips and exploring destinations.

Problem / Objective

The objective was to design a functional, aesthetically pleasing travel site that incorporates responsive imagery, semantic structure, and accessibility best practices. The challenge was to align the visual appeal with proper HTML organization and CSS responsiveness while ensuring validation compliance.

Process & Methodology

The project began with organizing semantic HTML structure for clarity and navigation. CSS Grid and Flexbox were used to create balanced layouts for mobile and desktop screens. Responsive <picture> elements were implemented to ensure optimized images for different screen sizes. The design used bright, inviting colors to evoke exploration and excitement.

Results & Outcome

The final product is a responsive and accessible website that delivers visual harmony, clear navigation, and strong usability. The travel theme is reinforced through consistent imagery and design tone. The site validates successfully and meets accessibility standards for modern web design.

Reflection

Wunderlust strengthened my technical understanding of responsive design and accessibility integration. It taught me how to balance aesthetic creativity with structure and functionality, ensuring inclusivity while maintaining visual impact.
